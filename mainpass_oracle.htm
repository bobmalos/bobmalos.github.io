One of the most fundamental ways to secure institutional data is by changing your password regularly. Security policy agreements require you to change your IDEA password on a regular basis. Here are some password guidelines:

  1. IDEA passwords must be managed solely by the authorized user of the IDEA ID.
  2. IDEA passwords must remain confidential.
  3. IDEA passwords must be changed periodically (e.g. at least every 90 days) and any time there is reason to suspect a password has been compromised.
  4. The best password is one that is not easily guessed but easy to remember.
  5. Random alpha-numeric passwords or an unusual word or combination of letters and numbers are the best passwords.
  6. Here are examples of password formats to avoid:
    • Passwords that are easily associated with yourself, family or pet names, nicknames, birthdates or telephone numbers.
    • Passwords that are the month of the year or day of the week.
    • Passwords that are all the same letters or numbers.
    • Previously used passwords.
  7. Don't post your password on or near your computer workstation or in any unsecured location.
